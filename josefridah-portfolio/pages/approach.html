Methodology

How I approach
every project.

I don't improvise. Every project I take on runs through a proven process — whether I'm the BA, the PM, or both.

How I Work

Process is not bureaucracy. It's how delivery doesn't fail.

Every methodology I use exists because I've seen what happens without it. Here's exactly how I approach projects as a TPM and as a BA.

As a Technical Project Manager

STEP 01
◈

Initiation & Governance Setup

Before a single sprint begins, I establish the governance framework that makes everything else possible. No project moves without a PID, risk register, and agreed acceptance criteria.

Project Initiation Document (PID)
Stakeholder map & RACI matrix
Initial risk register (RAG)
Budget baseline & CAPEX approval
PMO governance cadence set
STEP 02
◉

Requirements & Planning

I facilitate requirements workshops and work directly with BAs to produce a backlog that developers can actually build from — not vague user stories that cause rework.

Requirements elicitation workshops
SRS / BRD review & sign-off
Acceptance criteria authoring
Backlog grooming & sprint planning
Integration point identification
STEP 03
◆

Agile Delivery Execution

I run tight 2–3 week sprints with a consistent cadence — daily stand-ups, sprint demos, retrospectives. I remove blockers fast and protect the team from scope creep.

Daily stand-ups & blocker removal
Sprint reviews & retrospectives
Scope change impact assessment
Risk escalation management
Continuous stakeholder reporting
STEP 04
◎

QA, UAT & Release

I own the quality gate. UAT isn't an afterthought — it's planned from day one, with test scenarios mapped directly to acceptance criteria and business sign-off documented.

UAT test plan & scenario design
Sanity & regression testing
API integration testing (Postman)
Defect triage & resolution
Formal business sign-off

As a Business Analyst

BA STEP 01
▣

Discovery & Stakeholder Analysis

I start by understanding who has a stake in the outcome — not just the project sponsor. Every undocumented assumption is a future defect.

Stakeholder identification & mapping
Structured interviews & workshops
Pain point documentation
Business objectives vs. constraints
BA STEP 02
▤

As-Is Process Mapping

Before designing a solution, I document exactly how things work today — including the unofficial workarounds that never appear in process docs but drive 30% of the work.

Current-state workflow documentation
Gap & bottleneck analysis
Data flow mapping
System dependency identification
BA STEP 03
▥

SRS & Requirements Documentation

I produce SRS documents that serve as the single source of truth — functional requirements, non-functional requirements, acceptance criteria, and API integration specs all in one place.

Functional requirements (FR-XXX)
Non-functional requirements (NFR)
User stories with acceptance criteria
API integration requirements
Stakeholder sign-off & versioning
BA STEP 04
▦

UAT Planning & Sign-off

Requirements I've written become UAT test cases. I plan UAT from the start so there are no surprises at the end — and I own the business sign-off process through to completion.

UAT test plan & scenario matrix
Test data preparation
Defect logging & retesting
Business sign-off documentation
